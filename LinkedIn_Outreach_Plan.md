# LinkedIn Outreach Plan — General

Goal: 25 connection requests per session, 3-5 InMails. Part of the weekly 100-touch target.

---

## Step 1 — Sales Navigator filters

Broad. You're looking for small-mid business owners/ops people with problems you can solve.

Run multiple searches, mix and match:

**Search A — Operations / data pain:**
- Company size: 10-200 employees
- Geography: US (start with NYC/NJ/Miami, expand as needed)
- Job title: Owner OR CEO OR COO OR VP Operations OR Director of Operations
- Industry: any — construction, healthcare, food/bev, real estate, professional services

**Search B — Finance / billing pain:**
- Job title: CFO OR Controller OR Billing Manager OR Revenue Cycle
- Same company size and geography
- Industry: healthcare, home health, medical billing, insurance

**Search C — Your network's network:**
- Look at who your existing connections are connected to
- 2nd-degree connections from Solomon, Danny, Moshe, Sam = warm-adjacent

Pick 25 across all searches. Don't overthink it.

---

## Step 2 — Build a list of 25 prospects

Save to a Sales Navigator list (name it "Outreach March W2" or similar).
Note: name, title, company, why you picked them (1-2 words).

---

## Step 3 — Send 25 connection requests

- No message, or just "Hi [first name]"
- Do NOT pitch in the connection request
- Expected acceptance: 30-40% = ~10 connections over 3-5 days

---

## Step 4 — Pick 3-5 best fits for InMail

Use InMails on the strongest prospects — people who look like they have real ops pain.

Look for:
- People who post about business problems, hiring, scaling, or operations
- Companies in a growth phase (hiring, expanding) — more growth = more ops pain
- Industries where you have proof: healthcare billing, construction, food/bev, property mgmt

---

## Step 5 — Send the message

Pick the message that fits the person. You have three case studies — use the right one.

**For healthcare / billing people:**

> We built a system that caught $90k/year in missed insurance payments for a medical billing company in Brooklyn. They were matching ERA files to bank deposits by hand — 3+ hours a day.
>
> Happy to take a quick look at yours for free. If there's nothing to find, you lost 30 minutes.
>
> Want me to run it?

**For construction / trades / general business:**

> We set up an automated compliance and CRM system for a construction company that was tracking everything in spreadsheets. Cut their admin time in half.
>
> We do this for small businesses — automate the ops stuff that's eating your time. If you've got a process that feels like it should be easier, I'd be happy to take a look.
>
> What's your biggest ops headache right now?

**For anyone else:**

> We're a small dev team that automates operations for businesses in the 10-200 employee range. Data problems, manual processes, systems that don't talk to each other.
>
> Recent example: saved a Brooklyn medical practice 3 hrs/day by automating their payment reconciliation.
>
> If there's something in your business that feels harder than it should be, I'd be happy to take a quick look.

Keep it short. No "hope you're doing well." End with a question.

---

## Step 6 — As connections accept

Send the message the moment they accept. Don't batch. Don't wait. Speed matters.

## What to do with responses

**They reply with interest** — Ask what their biggest pain point is. Offer a free 30-min look.

**They describe a problem** — If you can solve it, say so. If you can't, say that too. Honesty builds trust.

**They say "not interested"** — Fine. Move on.

**They ignore you** — Normal. 80%+ will. Don't take it personally.

**They want to talk** — Book a call same week. Don't let it cool.

---

## Numbers

- 25 requests = ~10 connections = ~3-5 conversations = maybe 1 opportunity
- That's one session. Do this every Wednesday. Volume compounds.
- If 0 conversations after 2 weeks: change the message, not the volume.
