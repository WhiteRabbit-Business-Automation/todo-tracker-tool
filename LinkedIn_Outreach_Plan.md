# LinkedIn Outreach Plan — HHA Niche Test

Date: 2026-03-09 (Monday)
Goal: 25 connection requests out, 3-5 InMails sent. ~10 conversations by end of week.

---

## Step 1 — Open Sales Navigator and set filters

Go to Sales Navigator > Lead filters. Set:

- Industry: Home Health Care Services
- Company size: 20-200 employees
- Geography: New York, New Jersey
- Job title: Billing Manager OR CFO OR Owner OR Revenue Cycle OR Controller
- Company keywords: "home health" or "home care"

Billing managers are the #1 target — they do the work daily.
Owners at smaller agencies also feel it because it hits their wallet directly.

---

## Step 2 — Build a list of 25 prospects

Save results to a Sales Navigator list (name it "HHA Outreach March").
For each prospect note:

- Name
- Title
- Company name
- Company size (if visible)

Don't overthink filtering. If they match the filters, they're in. Move fast.

---

## Step 3 — Send 25 connection requests

Send a connection request to all 25. Either:

- No message at all (higher acceptance rate), or
- Just "Hi [first name]" — nothing else

Do NOT pitch in the connection request. 300 char limit makes it look spammy.

Expected acceptance rate: 30-40%. That gives you ~10 connections over 3-5 days.

---

## Step 4 — Pick 3-5 best fits for InMail

InMail goes directly to their inbox — no need to wait for connection acceptance.
You have a monthly InMail quota with your subscription. Use them on the best fits:

- Billing managers at agencies with 50-150 patients
- People who post about billing, revenue cycle, or home health operations
- Agencies you recognize or that look established (not brand new)

---

## Step 5 — Send the message

Same message for InMail and DMs (once connections accept). Copy-paste this:

> We built a system that caught $90k/year in missed insurance payments for a medical billing practice in Brooklyn. They were losing 3+ hours a day matching ERA files to bank deposits by hand.
>
> I'll do a free leak scan for your agency — send me one month of ERA files and bank statements, and I'll show you exactly what's falling through the cracks. If there's nothing, you lost 30 minutes.
>
> Want me to run it?

That's it. Don't add "hope you're doing well." Don't add your title. Don't add a PS.
Short, concrete, risk-free offer.

---

## Step 6 — As connections accept over the week

They'll trickle in over 3-5 days. When someone accepts:

1. Send the message above as a DM
2. That's it. Don't wait, don't batch — send it the moment you see the acceptance

## What to do with responses

**They reply with interest** — Send them instructions on what files to send.
You need: one month of ERA files (835 EDI or PDF remittance) + bank statements for the same month.

**They say "our software handles this"** — Great, that's intel. Ask: "Which system? Does it catch everything or do you still have manual cleanup?" Note what they say.

**They say "not interested"** — Fine. Move on. Don't follow up more than once.

**They ignore you** — Normal. Don't take it personally. 80%+ will ignore.

**They send files** — Run the scan immediately. Don't let it sit. Speed = trust.

---

## Numbers to remember

- 25 requests → ~10 connections → ~3-5 conversations → maybe 1 sends files
- That's a good week. If 0 conversations, change the message or the target title.
- This is week 1 of a 12-week test. Don't judge the niche off one week.
